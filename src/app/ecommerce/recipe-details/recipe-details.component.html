Rajma
Kidney Beans Curry

Rājmā, also known as rajmah, rāzmā, or lal lobia, is a vegetarian dish, originating from the Indian subcontinent, consisting of red kidney beans in a thick gravy with Indian whole spices, and is usually served with rice.

Ingrediants

1 x 2.5 cm piece / 10 g ginger

12 small cloves garlic

½ cup / 120 ml vegetable oil

3 whole dry Kashmiri red chillies

2 large / 300 g onions (grated)

3 large / 450 g tomatoes (chopped)

1 tbsp / 15 ml red chilli powder

1 tbsp +2½ tsp / 27.5 ml salt

2½ cups / 500 g rajma (soaked overnight or in hot water for 2 hours and drained)

4¼ cups / 1 litre water

1 tsp / 5 ml garam masala powder

Recipe
STEP 1/5

Grind together ginger and garlic into a paste

STEP 2/5

Heat oil in cooker about 3 minutes. Add chillies. Stir a few seconds. Add onions and fry till golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste. Stir a few seconds. Add tomatoes, chilli powder and salt. Cook till tomatoes are pulpy and oil shows separately (about 5 minutes), stirring occasionally. Add rajma and water. Mix.

STEP 3/5

Close cooker. Bring to full pressure on high heat. Reduce heat and cook 20 minutes.

STEP 4/5

Remove cooker from heat. Allow to cool naturally.

STEP 5/5

Open cooker. Partially mash rajma with back of a ladle till gravy is creamy. Stir in garam masala powder.

Serve hot.
